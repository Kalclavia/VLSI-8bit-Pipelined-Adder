# Lab 9: Logical-Effort Optimization

This lab analyzes 4-bit adder optimization using logical effort, with emphasis on power, die area, critical path behavior, and sizing tradeoffs. It provides a more analytical view of performance optimization than the earlier implementation-focused labs.

## Contents

- `Lab 9 Report.pdf` and `Lab 9 Report.docx`: Formal lab report documenting the logical-effort analysis and optimization results.
- Schematic and critical-path screenshots.
- Power and waveform screenshots for multiple sizing or optimization cases.
- `Abhishek_Bhattacharyya_Lab9_Submission.zip`: Original submitted project archive.

## What This Demonstrates

- Logical-effort analysis for digital circuit optimization.
- Critical-path identification and performance reasoning.
- Power measurement and comparison across design variants.
- Evaluation of power-vs-area tradeoffs.
- Analytical design optimization beyond simple functional correctness.

## Review Focus

Start with `Lab 9 Report.pdf`, then compare the waveform and power screenshots across the numbered cases. This lab is useful for showing tradeoff-driven engineering judgment: improving a design requires balancing speed, power, and physical area rather than optimizing a single metric in isolation.
